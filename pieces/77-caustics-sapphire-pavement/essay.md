# Like the Very Sky for Purity

## The Vision Beneath the Throne

"וַיִּרְאוּ אֵת אֱלֹהֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל וְתַחַת רַגְלָיו כְּמַעֲשֵׂה לִבְנַת הַסַּפִּיר וּכְעֶצֶם הַשָּׁמַיִם לָטֹהַר"

"And they saw the God of Israel; and under His feet was like the work of a sapphire pavement, and like the very heaven for purity." (Exodus 24:10)

Moses, Aaron, Nadab, Abihu, and the seventy elders ascended Sinai. They ate and drank in the divine presence. And the Torah pauses to describe one specific thing they perceived: not the face, not the voice, not the fire — but what lay *beneath*. Under the feet of the God of Israel was a pavement of sapphire, clear as the sky itself.

This verse is unique in the Torah. Theophany passages typically describe what surrounds or emanates from the divine: cloud, fire, thunder, the deep darkness. This one describes what is *underfoot*. The axis runs downward. The elders are looking at the floor.

## Two Readings, One Question

Medieval commentators split sharply on what this verse actually describes. Rashi, following Midrash Tanchuma, proposes that the sapphire pavement was not created at Sinai — it had been before God throughout the Egyptian slavery. Underneath the divine presence, the suffering of Israel was made visible: the Israelites had trodden clay bricks to build Pharaoh's cities; the sapphire evoked that labor, holding it in the divine field of awareness during the very moment of revelation. The pavement is not decorative. It is a memorial.

Nahmanides (Ramban) resists this reading. For him, "they saw" must be taken at face value. The verse describes something the elders actually perceived at the summit of Sinai — a literal effulgence of light and clarity beneath the divine presence. The Ramban's God is not symbolic; the sapphire is real, and the looking is real.

Both readings orbit the same question: what does it mean to *see* God, even partially, even obliquely, even from below?

Ibn Ezra offers a third angle. "Sapphire," he notes, may simply mean the sky — the same deep blue, the same quality of clear light. The pavement is not a gemstone but the heavens themselves, rendered underfoot. Sinai becomes the axis mundi: below, the mountain; above, the sky; and where the elders stood, the two met.

## The Physics of the Pavement

Caustics are what you see on the floor of a sunlit pool: shifting bright curves where refracted light has converged, surrounded by darker zones where it has been redirected away. They are produced when rays of light pass through a curved transparent surface — water, glass, ice — and bend according to Snell's law at each point on that surface. The surface's undulation focuses light here and defocuses it there, producing the characteristic shimmer.

This simulation renders exactly that process. A grid of 14,400 rays falls toward a gently undulating height field. At each point, the surface gradient deflects the ray. The landing positions accumulate energy in a buffer, which is then colorized: deep sapphire in the dark zones, cornflower blue at intermediate intensity, ice white at the caustic peaks where light converges.

The result is the pavement of Exodus 24:10 as a physical phenomenon, not a metaphor — "like the very sky for purity," because the sky is exactly the color of refracted blue light converging on a bright floor below.
