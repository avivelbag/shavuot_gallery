# 97 — Wherever You Go — Pursuit Curves of Ruth and Naomi

**Theme:** Book of Ruth
**Technique:** Discrete pursuit curves (mice problem)
**Year:** 2026

## Description

Three agents are placed at the vertices of an equilateral triangle. Ruth (warm
gold #C8922A) always pursues Naomi (ivory #F5EDE0), who remains stationary at
the center — the fixed destination. Orpah (terracotta #A05040) also pursues
Naomi initially, but after 30% of the convergence time her direction is smoothly
deflected outward over 60 frames, and she fades to zero opacity as she exits the
canvas. The full path of each agent accumulates on screen as a smooth polyline,
so the equiangular spirals grow visible over time. Once Ruth and Naomi meet, the
animation pauses for 2 seconds then fades and restarts seamlessly.

Behind the main narrative, forty-nine four-agent pursuit systems (square
configurations, step δ=0.3 px, wheat-gold palette at 15% opacity) fill the
canvas in a 7×7 grid. These loop continuously when all four agents converge,
evoking the 49 days of the Omer harvest season through which Ruth gleaned in
Boaz's fields.

## Theological Focus

Ruth 1:16–17 is the pivot of the Book of Ruth and one of the most complete
voluntary conversion statements in Tanakh. The Rabbis (Yevamot 47b) derive the
template for receiving converts from this episode. Pursuit curves enact the
logic of unconditional commitment: every step is directed straight at the target,
yet the path curves inexorably — through gleaning fields and threshing floors —
until arrival.

The forty-nine background systems evoke the Omer period: the harvest season,
the counting days, and the repeated pattern of dedication that turns wandering
into covenant.
