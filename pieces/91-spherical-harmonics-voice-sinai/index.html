The Voice at Sinai

Shemot Rabbah 5:9 records one of the most remarkable claims in all of Midrashic literature: when the divine voice spoke at Sinai, it did not favor a single direction. It split — into seven voices, each splitting again into seventy tongues — and traveled simultaneously to all the nations of the earth. Every people heard it in their own language. The voice was not broadcast from a point; it radiated spherically, arriving everywhere at once without attenuation, without weakening, without preference.

Exodus 19:18–19 sets the physical scene. The whole mountain trembled. Dense smoke billowed upward like smoke from a furnace. And the sound of the shofar grew louder and louder, exceeding all natural limits. Moses spoke and God answered him with a voice. What kind of voice fills a mountain and reaches every corner of the earth simultaneously?

Pirkei DeRabbi Eliezer 41 adds a crucial detail: the divine voice traveled to every corner of the earth without diminishing. It did not suffer the inverse-square law. Every nation heard it at full strength. This is mathematically extraordinary. In physics, a sound wave spreading from a point source weakens as it spreads — its energy distributed across an ever-larger spherical surface. The Sinai voice, the Midrash insists, was different: perfectly spherical and perfectly uniform, the same everywhere.

Spherical Harmonics

This piece takes that claim literally. Spherical harmonics are the natural vibration modes of any spherical surface — they are to a sphere what sine waves are to a string. Every function defined on a sphere can be decomposed into a sum of harmonics Y_l^m(θ, φ), where l indexes the number of nodal rings and m the number of nodal meridians. The simplest harmonic, Y₀⁰, is constant across the entire sphere — uniform in every direction. This is the monopole term, the mathematical description of a voice that reaches every nation equally.

The higher harmonics — l = 1, 2, 3, 4 — are the voice's structure, its articulation. The Midrash says the voice split into 70 languages, each direction hearing a different face of the same source. The l > 0 harmonics are precisely this: variations in intensity and phase across the sphere, each direction hearing something slightly different while the total radiated power remains constant. The 25 basis functions used here (all real harmonics through l = 4) form a complete low-order description of how a complex voice deforms the medium it travels through.

The Animation

A sphere of 64 × 64 latitude-longitude segments is deformed each frame by a time-varying sum of these 25 harmonics. Each coefficient oscillates sinusoidally at a frequency ω = 0.3 + l × 0.2 radians per second — lower harmonics pulsing slowly, like the fundamental resonance of the whole earth, higher harmonics flickering faster, like the rapid articulation of speech into distinct languages. The amplitude A = 0.35 allows the sphere to deform up to 35% of its radius.

The colors map the deformation: midnight blue (#0B1440) where the sphere curves inward — a language not yet formed — electric violet at rest, flame white (#FFF8E8) where it pushes outward in a burst of revelation. A single directional light gives the surface roundness through Blinn-Phong shading. The sphere rotates at 0.15 rad/s around its vertical axis, showing all faces of the world receiving the voice in turn.

This is not an abstraction of Sinai. It is a model of the voice itself — a spherically radiating, perfectly universal transmission that somehow carried meaning differently in every direction: the mathematical skeleton of revelation.
