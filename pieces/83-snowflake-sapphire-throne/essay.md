# The Essence of a Sapphire Brick

*"And they saw the God of Israel, and under His feet was like the work of a sapphire brick, and like the very heaven for clearness."*
— Exodus 24:10 (שמות כד:י)

This verse describes the most intimate divine encounter in the entire Torah. Moses, Aaron, Nadav, Avihu, and the seventy elders of Israel ascend Mount Sinai — not to receive the Torah, which comes later, but simply to see. They eat and drink in the presence of God (Exodus 24:11). What the text emphasizes is what they stand on: not rock, not cloud, but a crystalline pavement of sapphire, clear as the sky itself. The ground beneath divine presence is perfectly ordered.

The scene takes place in Exodus 24, before Moses's long forty-day ascent in chapters 25–31. The Torah has not yet been given in its written form. The elders stand on sapphire, see the divine presence, eat and drink, and are not harmed. The orderliness beneath God's feet is prior to the giving of the law.

The Sforno (Rabbi Ovadia Sforno, Italy, d. 1550) comments on this verse that the sapphire pavement represents the intellectual and moral clarity required to receive Torah. The ground of divine presence is without turbidity, perfectly transparent — the condition, in material form, for encountering the sacred.

The Talmud in Megillah 29a and Berachot 55a records that the Tablets of the Law were hewn from the sapphire stone beneath the Throne of Glory. They were not manufactured from earthly materials; they were substance from the divine realm itself, lowered into the physical world. This connects to a deeper tradition in Bereshit Rabbah 1:1: the Torah pre-existed Creation. God consulted the Torah as a blueprint when making the world. The sapphire pavement, then, is not metaphor — it is the material form of that pre-cosmic order.

This artwork renders that image literally. A single frozen seed at the center of a hexagonal diffusion field expands outward in six directions simultaneously, never deviating from the geometry encoded in the lattice. No randomness governs it. The six-fold symmetry is not chosen; it is the only form that the hexagonal topology allows. The crystal grows outward from a pre-existing law — one point of order crystallizing into visible form through a supersaturated field, each branch a fractal echo of the first, the whole structure a single sapphire growing from the ground of divine presence.
