# "Your People, My People" — Ruth's Road as Hilbert Curve

**Theme:** Book of Ruth / Loyalty and Journey — Ruth 1:16–17

**Technique:** Canvas 2D animated Hilbert curve, space-filling path

## Description

An order-6 Hilbert curve (4096 points on a 64×64 grid) is drawn incrementally, 8 segments per frame, tracing the entire canvas in a continuous space-filling path. The canvas is divided into two halves representing Moab (left, arid earth tones) and Bethlehem (right, harvest gold and wheat green). The curve's color transitions from dusty ochre on the left to harvest gold and wheat green as it crosses the midline.

When the full curve is drawn, the Hebrew text עַמֵּךְ עַמִּי (Ruth 1:16 — "Your people, my people") fades in at the top of the canvas. After a 3-second hold, the animation restarts from a blank canvas.

A faint vertical band marks the midline — the road and border between Moab and Bethlehem.
