# Moses Received and Transmitted — Möbius Chain of Tradition

**Theme:** Pirkei Avot 1:1 — the chain of oral tradition from Sinai through Joshua, the Elders, the Prophets, and the Men of the Great Assembly.

**Technique:** Möbius transformations / Schottky group / inversive geometry

## What this piece does

Three seed circles sit at the vertices of a slowly rotating equilateral triangle. For each adjacent pair of circles, a Möbius transformation is defined that maps the exterior of one to the interior of the next, forming a Schottky group. The group's generators and their inverses are iterated to depth 5, producing a cascade of hundreds of conformally equivalent image circles. Each circle in the cascade is a conformal copy of the original — same shape, preserved angles, shifted position — rendered in a warm gold-to-dark-red palette by generation depth.

The configuration rotates by 0.003 radians per frame, completing a full 2π turn in approximately 2094 frames (~35 seconds at 60 fps), then loops seamlessly.

## Shavuot connection

Pirkei Avot is traditionally studied between Pesach and Shavuot, one chapter per Shabbat. Its opening sentence — the five-link chain from Moses to the Men of the Great Assembly — establishes the epistemological warrant for the oral tradition: both Written and Oral Torah derive from the same Sinai revelation. Each Möbius image circle represents one generation of transmission: conformally equivalent (the teaching is preserved), positionally displaced (a new time and place). The limit points of the Schottky group, where circles shrink to dust, are the anonymous transmitters too distant to name — the horizon of history.
