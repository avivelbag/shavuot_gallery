# 51 — Let My Teaching Fall Like Rain

**Theme:** Torah as Water  
**Technique:** Raindrop particle simulation with ripple waves  
**Sources:** Deuteronomy 32:2, Isaiah 55:10–11, Taanit 7a, Bava Kamma 17a, Kiddushin 30b

Hebrew letters fall as rain from a night-sky canvas. Each letter represents one of the twenty-two letters of the Aleph-Bet. On impact with the ground line, each drop spawns an expanding circular ripple in pale gold, fading as it spreads. A pool of settled letters accumulates along the ground — faint glyphs suggesting the accumulation of Torah over generations.

The piece draws a deliberate contrast with the companion dew piece (37): dew is silent and gentle (Eruvin 54b), but rain is forceful and irresistible — as Taanit 7a compares a day of rain to the day of Matan Torah. The Sinai event was not a quiet offering but an overwhelming downpour.
