# And the Mountain Burned — de Jong Attractor

**Theme:** Har Sinai / Matan Torah — the theophany at Sinai (Exodus 19)

**Technique:** de Jong strange attractor / density accumulation

One million iterated points from the de Jong map (*x' = sin(a·y) − cos(b·x), y' = sin(c·x) − cos(d·y)*) accumulate into a Float32Array density buffer, tone-mapped logarithmically to a fire palette (ember → orange → gold-white) on a near-black ground. Three parameter presets cycle over ~30 seconds, dissolving between a tall flame column, wide billowing cloud, and narrow pillar — evoking the cloud by day and fire by night that led Israel to Sinai (Exodus 13:21).

The essay cites Exodus 19:16 and 19:18 (the thunder, shofar, fire, and smoke of a furnace), Exodus 13:21 (cloud and fire), and the Talmud (Shabbat 88b) on the mountain held over Israel's heads. The mathematical theme: local determinism producing globally irreducible emergence, mirroring how revelation at Sinai arrived through lawful preparation yet was genuinely unprecedented.
