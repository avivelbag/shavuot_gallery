# 54 — Forty-Nine Gates

**Theme:** Sefirat HaOmer — The Omer as Curriculum of Self  
**Technique:** Procedural grid / generative mandala (canvas 2D bezier petals, continuous rotation)

A 7×7 canvas grid where each of the 49 cells draws a unique geometric mandala driven by its
kabbalistic Sefirah pair. The rotational symmetry order is determined by the inner (daily) Sefirah,
the colour hue by the outer (weekly) Sefirah, and the ring density by the day within its week.

All mandalas bloom simultaneously on load and rotate continuously — alternating direction by week —
creating a living clockwork of self-refinement. Hovering a cell reveals the Hebrew day number, the
week/day in English, and the Sefirah pair name.

The Shavuot element is rendered separately below the grid, outside the 49-cell matrix, as a golden
8-petalled mandala that pulses and slowly rotates. It cannot sit inside the 7×7 structure — the
count ends at 49, and the fiftieth day stands beyond all counting.

**Essay angle:** The Omer as *cheshbon hanefesh* (spiritual accounting) — each sefirah pair as a
diagnostic lens for self-examination, based on the Sfat Emet's reading of *שַׁבָּתוֹת תְּמִימֹת*
("complete Sabbaths") as a call to intentional daily practice.
