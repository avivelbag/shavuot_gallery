# 60 — Livnat HaSapir: The Pavement of Sapphire

**Theme:** Har Sinai — the sapphire pavement vision at the covenant ratification

**Technique:** Wang tiles / aperiodic mosaic — greedy edge-matching on a 20×20 canvas grid

When Moses, Aaron, Nadab, Abihu, and seventy elders ascended Sinai in Exodus 24:9–11 to ratify the covenant, they saw the God of Israel, and under His feet was something like a pavement of sapphire, clear as the sky itself. This piece renders that vision as an animated Wang-tile mosaic: tiles with colored edges (deep sapphire, sky blue, pale ice, gold) expand outward from the center of a 700×700 canvas, one tile per frame, filling the grid in ~8 seconds before fading and restarting with a new random seed.

The central 3×3 void holds the Hebrew phrase *תַּחַת רַגְלָיו* ("under His feet"), marking the site of the vision without depicting what stood above it.

**Source:** Exodus 24:9–11; Rashi and Nachmanides on 24:10
