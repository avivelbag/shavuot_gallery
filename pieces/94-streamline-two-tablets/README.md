# Between the Tablets, the Law Flows

**Theme:** Luchot habrit / Ten Commandments — Shavuot and the giving of the Torah  
**Technique:** Magnetic dipole vector field streamlines (RK4 integration)

Two magnetic dipoles placed symmetrically — one for each luchot (tablet) — produce
field lines that arch between them in the shape of a Torah crown, and fan outward in
rays reminiscent of divine rays descending on Sinai.

The electric-blue streamlines are traced via fourth-order Runge-Kutta integration of
the 2D dipole field equation: from seed points on a small ring around each pole the
algorithm steps forward along the field direction until the path hits a pole or exits
the canvas. Seed rings rotate at 2°/second so the pattern slowly breathes — an
animation of living law.

The field lines never cross, which is a mathematical theorem, and also a metaphor:
the obligation toward God (commandments I–V on the left tablet) and the obligation
toward neighbor (VI–X on the right tablet) cannot contradict each other.
